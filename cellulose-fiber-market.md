<p><ul><li>Natural Cellulose Fibers</li><li>Man-Made Cellulose Fibers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cellulose fiber market is categorized into two main types: natural cellulose fibers and man-made cellulose fibers. Natural cellulose fibers, derived from plants such as cotton, hemp, and flax, are prized for their biodegradability and comfort. In contrast, man-made cellulose fibers, produced through chemical processes from regenerated cellulose, include materials like viscose and lyocell. These fibers offer versatility and can be engineered for specific properties, catering to various applications in textiles, automotive, and non-woven products.</p></p>
